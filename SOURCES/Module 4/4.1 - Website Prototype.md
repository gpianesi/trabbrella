
A **website prototype** is a preliminary model of a website that represents its proposed **layout**, **visual design**, and **interactive features**, including navigation and specific functionalities.  

Prototypes are used during the process of **requirements gathering** and **feedback refinement**, allowing multiple stakeholders to evaluate and improve the design before full development begins.

This stage is critical for:
- Clarifying project goals  
- Identifying usability issues early  
- Reducing development time and cost  
- Ensuring efficient and goal-oriented implementation  

---

## Prototype Progression

Website design typically evolves through the following stages:

### 1. Wireframe
- Focuses on **structure and layout**
- Uses simple shapes and placeholders
- No visual styling or branding
- Emphasizes content placement and user flow

### 2. Mockup
- Adds **visual design elements**
- Includes colors, typography, and imagery
- Represents the look and feel of the final product
- Usually non-interactive

### 3. Prototype
- Combines **visual design and interaction**
- Simulates navigation and user actions
- Used for testing functionality and usability
- Closely resembles the final website behavior

## Approaches, Methods, and Tools in Web Prototyping

### Paper Prototyping
- Low-fidelity, hand-drawn representations
- Allows **rapid iteration** and experimentation
- Ideal for early-stage brainstorming
- Cost-effective and easy to modify

### Prototyping Software
- Dedicated digital tools for interactive prototypes
- Common examples:
  - **Figma**
  - **Axure RP**
- Supports:
  - Clickable navigation
  - Animations and transitions
  - Collaborative design workflows

### Human-Centered Design (HCD)
- A design approach focused on **end-user needs**
- Involves users throughout the design process
- Emphasizes:
  - Continuous feedback
  - Usability testing
  - Iterative refinement
- Improves accessibility, satisfaction, and effectiveness

---

## Key Takeaway

Prototyping is a foundational step in web development that bridges the gap between ideas and implementation. By progressing from wireframes to interactive prototypes and incorporating user feedback early, teams can create more effective, usable, and successful websites.
