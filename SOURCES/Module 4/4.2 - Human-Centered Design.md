
**Human-Centered Design (HCD)** is an approach to problem-solving that actively involves the **people for whom a solution is being developed**. These humans—typically **end users** or individuals affected by the solution—are engaged **at every stage of the design process**.

The core goal of HCD is to ensure that solutions are:
- Relevant to real user needs  
- Usable and accessible  
- Effective within real-world contexts  

---

## Stages of Human-Centered Design

Human-Centered Design follows an **iterative process**, where insights from users continuously inform design decisions.

## Four Principles of Human-Centered Design

### 1. **Focus on People**
- Identify **who** will use the product  
- Understand **why** they will use it  
- Analyze **how and in what context** it will be used  

### 2. **Find and Understand the Right Problem**
- Clearly define the **actual user problem**, not just assumed issues  
- Base problem definitions on real user insights  

### 3. **Think in Terms of a System**
- Consider the **broader environment** in which the product operates  
- Account for technical, social, and organizational factors  

### 4. **Test Solutions with Real People**
- Gather feedback from users at **each iteration**  
- Use testing results to refine and improve the design

## Applying HCD Principles in Web Development

Human-Centered Design can be effectively applied to websites and web applications through the following practices:

### User Research and Empathy
- Conduct:
  - **Interviews**
  - **Surveys**
  - **Behavioral analysis**
- Develop:
  - **Personas** to represent target users
  - **Storyboards** to map user experiences

### Problem Definition
- Identify and clearly articulate the **user problems** the website or web app aims to solve  
- Align design goals with real user needs  

### Collaborative Prototyping
- Involve users in **co-design and prototyping sessions**
- Encourage feedback during early design stages  

### Iterative Testing
- Test successive versions of the product with users  
- Continuously refine based on usability findings  

---

## Key Takeaway

By embedding users throughout the design and development process, Human-Centered Design ensures that web solutions remain **usable, meaningful, and aligned with human needs**, resulting in higher-quality and more successful digital products.
